Textbooks:
We will not use a fixed book. But if you feel like buying one for collection, I would recommend Abstract Algebra, 3rd Edition by D. S. Dummit and R. M. Foote, which contains a lot more material than that can be covered in a single course (which, of course, may be useful for research in the future if you are into that kind of nerdy stuff...). Another great reference is M. Artin's algebra book, which is less dry than the above but contains less material.
