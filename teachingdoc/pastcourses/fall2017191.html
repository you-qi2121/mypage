Course Description:
This course is aimed at giving a gentle introduction to Khovanov homology. We will discuss Khovanov's categorification of the Jones polynomial and its extension to tangles and tangle-cobordisms. We will also explain Khovanov-Lee homology and Rasmussen's proof of the Milnor conjecture.
Course Documents:
Here we will collect some references as the course moves along.
For the first part of the course, we will be mainly talking about Khovanov homology, and the best reference is Khovanov's original papers.
[1] A categorification of the Jones polynomial, Duke Math. J. 101 (2000), no. 3, 359-426, arXiv:math/9908171.
This is the orginal construction of Khovanov homology categorifying the Jones polynomial for knots and links.

[2] A functor-valued invariant of tangles, Algebr. Geom. Topol. 2 (2002), 665-741, arXiv:math/0103190.
In this papaer, the construction in [1] is extened to tangles, resulting in a functor-valued invariant.

[3] An invariant of tangle cobordisms, Trans. Amer. Math. Soc. 358 (2006), 315-327, arXiv:math/0207264.
This paper shows that the invariant obtained in [1] and [2] are (projective) functorial with respect to tangle cobordisms.

A very readable set of notes is written by Khovanov and Asaeda for the Park City meeting:
[4] Notes on link homology, arXiv:0804.1279.

On variations of Khovanov homology.

The following is the work of Bar-Natan on a t-deformed version of Khovanov homology, which leads to Khovanov-Lee homology groups.
[5] D. Bar-Natan, On Khovanov's categorification of the Jones polynomial, Algebraic & Geometric Topology 2 (2002) 337-370, arXiv:math/0201043.

[6] E. S. Lee, An endomorphism of the Khovanov invariant, Advances in Mathematics, Volume 197, Issue 2, (2005), Pages 554-586, arXiv:math/0210213.

The next work is an algebraic/topological-field-theoretical "explanation" of Bar-Natan's deformation, as well as some further generalization. The last section of the paper discusses Lee's homology groups without using spectral sequences.

[7] M. Khovanov, Link homology and Frobenius extensions, Fundamenta Mathematicae, 190 (2006), 179-190, arXiv:math/0411447.

Rasmussen's combinatorial proof of the Milnor Conjecture using Khovanov-Lee homology.

[8] J. A. Rasmussen, Khovanov homology and the slice genus, Inventiones Mathematicae, Volume 182, Issue 2, (2010), pp 419-447, arXiv:math/0402131.

Further reading:
Here are some related topics to this course. The center of the arc algebras is isomorphic to the cohomology ring of a certain Springer variety:
[1]M. Khovanov, Crossingless matchings and the cohomology of (n,n) Springer varieties, Communications in Contemporary Math. 6 (2004) no.2, 561--577, arXiv:math/0202110.

The above phenomenon is more categorical. Certain constructible sheaves supported on the Springer fiber describes a certain parabolic category O of sl(2n). The center of these categories are isomorphic to the cohomology ring of the Springer fibers. The results are proven, independently, by Jon Brundan and Catharina Stroppel.
[2] J. Brundan, Centers of degenerate cyclotomic Hecke algebras and parabolic category O, Represent. Theory 12 (2008), 236-259, arXiv:math/0607717.

[3]C. Stoppel, Perverse Sheaves on Grassmannians, Springer Fibres and Khovanov cohomology, Compositio Math. 145 (2009), 954-992, arXiv:math/0608234.

Khovanov's arc algebra has a subquotient variant that categorifies a tensor product of the two-dimensional module over sl(2):
[4] Y. Chen and M. Khovanov, An invariant of tangle cobordisms via subquotients of arc rings, arXiv:math/0610054.

A more algebro-topological direction: the following beautiful paper of Lipshitz-Sakar constructs a spectrum for a given link whose homology is the Khovanov homology, allowing one to compute Steenrod operations on Khovanov homology:
[5] A Khovanov homotopy type, J. Amer. Math. Soc. 27 (2014), 983-1042, arXiv:1112.3932.

Finally, a similar construction by Khovanov on sl(3) link homology using foams:
[6] M. Khovanov, sl(3) link homology I, Algebr. Geom. Topol. 4 (2004) 1045-1081, arXiv:math/0304375.
